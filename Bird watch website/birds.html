Birds of conservation concern in Ireland

There are a number of birds in Ireland whose number are in decline. Some of those with high priority for conservation are:

Barn owl

The barn owl is the most common owl and is found in most parts of the world. It has seen a huge decline in Ireland and Europe in recent years

Curlew

The curlew is recongnisable by its long curved bill

Curlews use their long bills to search for worms in the mud or very soft ground
